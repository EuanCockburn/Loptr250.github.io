Glossary

Administrator:

The person responsible for managing and, often, maintaining a system. Some systems, particullarly larger ones, can have multiple administrators.

Authentication:

The act of verifying an identity with the computer.

Authentication secret:

The secret answer to an authorisation question. This could be a text based password, the image selected as the correct one for an image selection authentication, or even a finger print reading.

Authorisation:

A level of access assigned to a person to decide what they can access and what is off limits.

Biometrics:

The analysis of biological features of a person.

DataBase:

A structure used by a computer to store large amounts of data to be called on later.

Guest:

A user referred to as a guest is, typically, someone who does not own the device and has no permission to access vital components or even most of the file system. Their access is normally extremely restricted.

I.D.:

An abbreviation for Identity Document, it refers to any piece of documentation that may be used to identify you. These include passports and driving licenses. In terms of computers an I.D. is a unique identifier used to recognise certain components or articular users.

Identification:

The act of supplying your identity to someone or something.

Iris:

The coloured section of an eye that surrounds the pupil.

Observe

To watch or witness.

Retina:

The layer at the back of the eyeball. It contains cells sensitive to light that fire off electrical impulses to the brain when stimulated.

Username:

A value normally used by websites to identify a user.

Validate:

Confirm the correctness of something. In the case of authentication to confirm the correctness of your given identity.
